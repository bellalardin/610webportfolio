All course descriptions taken in part from the official course catalog of the University of Pittsburgh.

Engineering (General)

ENGR 0501: Music Engineering Laboratory

Introduction to recording engineering supplemented with lectures on the engineering of music. Learned how to record, edit, and produce tracks within Digital Performer software, basics of recording engineering, and how to use equipment around the lab. Learned about the physics of music and mechanical/electronic function of musical instruments.

ENGR 0716: Art of Hands-On System Design Engineering

Hands-on introductory design course with a focus on user-centric design principles. Learned basic engineering skills including soldering, Arduino coding, drilling, tapping, and design using automated systems. Researched current engineering issues, conducted in-depth user outreach and testing, delivered weekly presentations, created sketches and models of designs, completed several short-term and long-term design projects, both individually and with teams. Projects can be viewed here.

Electrical Engineering

ECE 0101: Linear Circuits and Systems

Introduction to linear circuit analysis. Learned basic electrical engineering concepts and used methods such as mesh current analysis, nodal voltage analysis, Thevenin and Norton equivalent circuits, and Kirchoff's and Ohm's laws to solve a variety of DC and AC circuits. Used voltmeters, waveform generators, oscilloscopes, and other lab equipment to build and analyze various circuits.

ECE 0102: Microelectronic Circuits

Continuation of ECE 0101. Covered the fundamentals of microelectronic circuits, studying operational amplifiers, diode and rectifier circuits, analysis and design of MOS field-effect transistor and bipolar-junction transistor amplifiers, and CMOS digital-logic circuits. Designed and analyzed various circuits using these components in the lab.

ECE 0201: Digital Circuits and Systems

Introduction to digital circuit design. Studied boolean algebra, binary and hexadecimal numbers, a variety of combinational and sequential logic circuits, and finite state machines. Learned basic breadboarding skills and basics of VHDL. Designed simple circuits using breadboards and chips, as well as more complex circuits using VHDL, including counters, decoders, and finite state machines.

ECE 0202: Embedded Processors and Interfacing

Course covering the fundamentals of embedded processors and interfacing. Studied the structure of various microprocessors and their instruction sets, wrote several programs in C and assembly, and designed built simple circuits in C and Assembly using STM32 Nucleo-64 development board.

ECE 0301: ECE Problem Solving With C++

Introductory C++ course. Topics covered include loops, files, functions, pointers, vectors, classes, inheritance, and polymorphism, among other basic C++ concepts. Created programs to evaluate logic statements, perform operations using Boolean algebra, perform matrix-vector multiplication, analyze basic circuits, create sinusoidal signals, and perform sorting algorithms. Analyzed real-life case studies related to the IEEE code of ethics and discussed how to be an ethical engineer.

ECE 0302: Data Structures and Algorithms

Course covering various abstract data types including stacks, queues, trees, lists, and heaps. Wrote several programs in C++ based on pre-existing specifications using these abstract data types and designed and ran tests on these programs.

ECE 0401: ECE Analytical Methods

Course covering various mathematical concepts related to the fields of electrical and computer engineering. Topics covered include matrices, vectors, systems of equations, determinants, double integrals, first- and second-order ordinary differential equations, complex numbers, Eulers identity, phasor analysis, signal waveforms, Laplace transform, transfer functions, frequency response, and an introduction to the Fourier transform. Solved several problems related to these topics both on paper and within MATLAB.

ECE 0402: Signals, Systems, & Probability

Course covering the mathematical analysis of signals and input-output systems. Applied knowledge of differential equations and Laplace transforms to analysis of signals and systems and introduced Fourier transforms and basic concepts in probability. Wrote MATLAB scripts used to analyze various signals.

English Writing & Composition

ENGCMP 0210: Seminar in Composition - Engineering

Introdutory writing course specific to engineering concepts. Analyzed codes of ethics and ethical case studies, wrote persuasive and technical essays, used University library resources to research topics for essays.

ENGCMP 0610: Composing Digital Media

Class focused on the creation, design and analysis of various types of digital media. Designed audio projects and visual projects within Audacity and GIMP software, coded websites within HTML and CSS, learned auditory, visual, and UX design concepts and analyzed various types of digital media using these concepts.
